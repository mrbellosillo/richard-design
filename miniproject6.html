UI AUDIT
UI Redesign & Principles Audit
OBJECTIVE
Audit an existing UI for violations of core principles and redesign using improved color and typography systems.
OVERVIEW

Selected an old design with significant design flaws and audited at least three screens against core UI/UX principles. The process involved documenting violations, then delivering a high-fidelity redesign with clear before/after justifications. Key improvements included dark mode surface treatment (darkest blue instead of pure black), transparency on login card elements, and replacing harsh chart colors with harmonious palette alternatives.

KEY HIGHLIGHTS
  • Documented at least three major UI/UX principle violations with structured written justifications
  • Used the darkest shade of blue instead of pure black for authentic dark mode surfaces
  • Applied effective card transparency to enhance visual depth without sacrificing legibility
  • Replaced harsh red/green chart colors with softer, palette-aligned alternatives

DESIGN LEAD FEEDBACK

Dark mode effectively uses the darkest shade of blue instead of pure black — a suitable choice. The transparency level on the login card is effective. Red and green graph colors are too harsh — remove or replace with softer shades.

REFLECTION

An audit is only as useful as the redesign it produces. The discipline of documenting flaws before fixing them changed how I evaluate interfaces — I now see problems before reaching for solutions.
