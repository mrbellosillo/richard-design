LAYOUT PATTERNS
F-Pattern & Z-Pattern Layouts
OBJECTIVE
Apply F-pattern and Z-pattern reading flow principles to design 4 screens (2 website, 2 mobile) using the moodboard theme.
OVERVIEW

Designed four screens using directional reading pattern layouts — F-pattern for the first set (website + mobile) and Z-pattern for the second set. All designs adhered to the mood board theme from the previous session, integrating the established color system, typography, and grid. The F-pattern layout was accepted on the first submission. The Z-pattern required one revision to better align with the standard structure, which was successfully completed.

KEY HIGHLIGHTS
  • Applied F-pattern for content-heavy, scannable layouts with strong left-edge anchoring
  • Structured Z-pattern for clean visual flow across sparse, high-impact pages
  • Integrated moodboard color system, Lato typography, and spacing consistently across all 4 screens
  • Revised Z-pattern arrangement to fully align with standard pattern structure after feedback
FINAL DESIGNS
F-PATTERN
Z-PATTERN
DESIGN LEAD FEEDBACK

F-pattern layout is clear and effective. Choice of elements, colors, and background for Z-pattern is appropriate — layout needs refinement to adhere to standard Z-pattern structure.

REFLECTION

Pattern-based layouts aren't rigid rules — they're a way of thinking about how eyes move through a page. Knowing when to use F vs Z changes how you prioritize and position content before any visual styling begins.
